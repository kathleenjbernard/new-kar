Audio Engineer

Let's transform your track!

Getting your music to its final form doesn't have to be complicated. My mixing process follows four simple steps:

Upload

View instructions Once your music is recorded the way you want it, bounce all your tracks out separately, zip them in a folder, and share them via Google Drive or Dropbox using kurtis.a.roy@gmail.com. It's also helpful for me if you include a few inspiration songs to give me a clearer direction of your goals.

If you're using Logic Pro:
You can send the session files to me in a zip folder. Make sure to delete all unused project files to reduce the size.

If sending from another DAW:
It's really helpful for me if all your stems start and stop at the same time. For example, if you have a guitar solo happening at 2:30, bounce the track so there's an empty waveform block starting at the beginning and ending at 2:30. This is a fool-proof way to ensure everything lines up correctly and saves a lot of time.

Mix

View instructions I will organize and clean up your stems, perform any quantization on the drums and other instruments (if needed) and tune your vocals if requested. Then, I will transform your track from a rough mix to a polished first pass. Once the first pass is complete, it is run through a variety of speakers to ensure it will translate well no matter the output. Then, it is sent to you for feedback!

Revise

View instructions Music is subjective, and as much as we both would like to nail the first pass, this typically never happens - and that's ok! I am not a "what I say goes" engineer - I want to hear ALL your opinions! I offer unlimited mix revisions in order to ensure that we both are satisfied.

With that in mind, please understand that there are certain elements I can't control - such as how the track was produced and recorded. No amount of mixing can fix recording errors.

Download

View instructions After discussing and completing mix revisions, I will send you a high quality .WAV file containing your finished mix. Your mix will be at the appropriate levels and contain enough headroom to ensure that a mastering engineer can do their best work. The rest is up to you!

Frequently Asked Questions

What's your turnaround time?
This depends on how large your project is and how many tracks you’re working on. For singles, I’d say about a week. For EPs and albums, that can take anywhere from a few weeks to a month. If you need a mix by a deadline, let me know and we can work that out.
What genres do you usually mix?
My music taste leans on the “indie rock” side, so that is a genre I’m very comfortable with. However, I have mixed quite a bit of music from other genres (hip hop, americana, bluegrass, and reggaeton) so I can handle basically any genre!
Can you tune my vocals?
Yes, I can! Just know that tuning sounds the most natural when you capture good vocal takes the first time around. Fixing it in the mix can only go so far, so make sure you’re getting close to perfect as possible before mixing!
Can you quantize my drums?
Yes! However, similar to tuning vocals, quantizing can only work when you try to get as close to perfect as you can. Sloppy drum performances can only be tightened up so much without it being noticeable.
What kind of gear do you use?
I tend to mix a lot in the box these days, as recalling is so much easier - and plugins have dramatically improved. I do love to use my IRL hairball 1176 on mostly everything, but specifically on vocals, bass and drums to add that 3D compression. Other gear I have includes:
  • Monitors: Focal Twins, Avantone
  • Mixcube Converters: Focusrite Clarett
  • Outboard Gear: Rev D 1176 from Hairball Audio, Rev A 1176 from Hairball Audio.
  • Preamps: Hairball Audio Lolas + DITRE Color pre
  • Plugins: I use Slate/Waves plugins - I find them to actually do something!
  • Microphones: 2 AKG 414 XLS, Oktava Mk12;s, Cascade Fathead II, Shure Sm57’s, Audio Technica 4040
  • Instruments: Frankenstrat w/ texas special pickups, Fender J-Bass, 1997 Squier Supersonic, Roland JC40 Amplifer
Can you also master?
I don’t typically like to master songs I’ve mixed, as it is bad practice to try and “change” the sound of something I’ve worked on with the same room, speakers and ears. I highly recommend you take your mixes to a real mastering engineer. That said, (for an additional charge) I can do my best and send you a master of your track.
I want to learn more about mixing. Can I do an internship with you?
While I love sharing the knowledge I’ve learned from experience, most of my work is mixing in my home studio, which doesn’t really lend itself to a super interesting experience. I typically work alone, but I’m always open to sharing the things I’ve learned along the way. Shoot me an email and maybe we can work something out.
