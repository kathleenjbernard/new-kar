Questions

What's your turnaround time?
This depends on how large your project is and how many tracks you're working on. For singles, I'd say about a week. For EPs and albums, that can take anywhere from a few weeks to a month. If you need a mix by a deadline, let me know so I can accommodate that!
What genres do you usually mix?
All kinds! I have the most experience working with Indie rock/pop, but I have mixed music from many other genres, including Hip Hop, Americana, Bluegrass, and Reggaeton.
Can you tune my vocals?
Yes! Just know that tuning sounds the most natural when you capture good vocal takes the first time around. Fixing it in the mix can only go so far, so make sure you’re getting close to perfect as possible before mixing.
Can you quantize my drums?
Yes! However, similar to tuning vocals, quantizing can only work when you try to get as close to perfect as you can.
What kind of gear do you use?
Hairball Audio is my go-to for a lot of my gear - especially when working on vocals, bass and drums. Other gear I have includes:
  • Monitors: Focal Twins, Avantone
  • Mixcube Converters: Focusrite Clarett
  • Outboard Gear: Rev D 1176 from Hairball Audio, Rev A 1176 from Hairball Audio.
  • Preamps: Hairball Audio Lolas + DITRE Color pre
  • Plugins: I use Slate/Waves plugins
  • Microphones: 2 AKG 414 XLS, Oktava Mk12s, Cascade Fathead II, Shure Sm57s, Audio Technica 4040
  • Instruments: Frankenstrat w/ texas special pickups, Fender J-Bass, 1997 Squier Supersonic, Roland JC40 Amplifer
Can you also master?
I can, but I usually don't like to master tracks that I've mixed - it's not best practice to modify the sound of something I've already worked on with the same room, speakers and ears. I recommend having an experienced mastering engineer master your tracks for the best possible results. If you are just looking to get your mixes loud for digital release, I can do that for you - although this isn’t really considered "mastering." I'm happy to offer suggestions on mastering engineers if you need them!
I want to learn more about mixing. Can I do an internship with you?
While I love sharing my passion for mixing with others, most of my work is done in my home studio, which doesn’t lend itself to a super interesting experience. But I’m always open to sharing the things I’ve learned along the way! Send me an email with what you have in mind.
